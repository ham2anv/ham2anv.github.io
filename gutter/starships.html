Technologies

Hyperdrive

The only method of FTL travel known to humanity is the hyperdrive engine. Ships have hyperdrive systems rated in FTL units. A ship can travel [2(FTL - 2) + 1] parsecs per week. For simplicity, consult the following table.

FTL Parsecs/week
1 1.5
2 2
3 3
4 5
5 9
6 17

Hyperdrives require two things: power, supplied by the starship's power plant; and "fuel," typically a gas like hydrogen or helium, used to form a stable bubble around the ship in hyperspace. Most starships carry enough fuel for 1 week of travel at maximum FTL. A ship traveling at less than its maximum FTL can stay in hyperspace proportionally longer.

Example: The Ticonderoga has an FTL-3 hyperdrive system. It needs to travel 2 parsecs. At FTL-3, this would take 4⅔ days, leaving the ship with 2⅓ days of fuel. However, at FTL-1, the journey takes over 9 days, but the Ticonderoga reaches its destination with nearly 4 days of fuel left.
